## Recommendation: Unifying Masked Diffusion Models with Various Generation Orders and Beyond

- **Paper:** [Unifying Masked Diffusion Models with Various Generation Orders and Beyond](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.02112)
- **Domain:** Diffusion language models / masked discrete diffusion
- **Authors:** Chunsan Hong, Sanghyun Lee, Jong Chul Ye
- **Posted:** 2026-02-02; revised 2026-05-21
- **Validation signal:** Accepted at ICML 2026; author/project pages report spotlight status. Citation signal is still early, so this is mainly a venue-plus-content pick rather than a citation-momentum pick.

### Recommendation Summary

This is today's best diffusion LM read because it treats generation order as a central modeling problem, not just a decoding-time heuristic. The paper unifies autoregressive, standard masked diffusion, and blockwise generation under an order-expressive masked diffusion framework, then proposes LoMDM to learn context-dependent generation order jointly with the diffusion backbone. That makes it directly relevant to the current question of whether diffusion LMs can win by exploiting flexible generation orders rather than merely imitating AR models with more parallelism.

### Why It Matters Now

Recent diffusion LM work is increasingly about inference schedules, unmasking policies, and speed-quality tradeoffs. This paper is useful because it pushes that thread into the training objective itself: if generation order matters, the model should learn the order jointly instead of relying on fixed schedules or post-hoc policies.

### Core Idea

- Generalize masked diffusion so many generation orders, including AR and block diffusion, fit inside one probabilistic framework.
- Learn a context-dependent unmasking/order policy together with token prediction from scratch.
- Show improvements over several discrete diffusion baselines on language modeling benchmarks, suggesting generation order is a real modeling lever.

### Broader Trend

This belongs to the shift from "masked diffusion as parallel decoding" toward "masked diffusion as flexible-order generation." The field is converging on the idea that DLM quality and efficiency depend heavily on which tokens are committed when, whether through learned order, adaptive decoding, speculative decoding, or token-level early stopping.

### Read Carefully / Skepticism

The key question is whether LoMDM's gains survive at larger model and data scales, and whether learned ordering improves practical wall-clock generation enough to matter against strong AR and semi-AR baselines. Also check whether benchmark improvements come from genuinely better ordering or from extra training/objective complexity that may be hard to amortize.
