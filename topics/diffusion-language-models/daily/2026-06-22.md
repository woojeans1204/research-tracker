### Why This Paper

This is worth reading now because it asks a central 2026 question for diffusion LMs: whether masked diffusion is actually the dominant path, or whether uniform-state and interpolating diffusion methods scale better once practical sampling tradeoffs are included. The paper stands out for comparing diffusion families at scale, emphasizing FLOPs, likelihood, sampling speed, and downstream quality rather than relying on perplexity alone.

### Core Idea

- Compare masked, uniform-state, and interpolating discrete diffusion language models through scaling-law and speed-quality analyses.
- Show that perplexity can be misleading across diffusion families, since models with worse likelihood scaling may sample faster or perform better in practical settings.
- Scale methods up to 1.7B parameters and report that uniform-state diffusion remains competitive and can outperform AR and masked diffusion baselines on GSM8K after supervised fine-tuning.

### Critical Notes

- Treat the GSM8K result as suggestive rather than a broad reasoning claim until replicated across more tasks and training recipes.
- Check how much the conclusions depend on implementation details, sampler choice, and compute allocation.
- The citation signal is still early because the paper is only from February 2026; the venue/project/code signals matter more right now.
