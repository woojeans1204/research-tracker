## Scaling Beyond Masked Diffusion Language Models

- Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.15014
- Domain: Diffusion language models
- Source: arXiv, ICML 2026 signal from project/repository pages
- Recommended by: Reasoning & Diffusion LM Paper Scout
- Priority: high
- Topic tags: diffusion-language-models, discrete-diffusion, masked-diffusion, scaling-laws, evaluation, inference-efficiency
- Broader trend: moving diffusion LM evaluation beyond masked-only objectives and beyond perplexity-only comparison toward speed-quality and scaling tradeoffs

### Why This Paper

This is worth reading now because it asks a central 2026 question for diffusion LMs: whether masked diffusion is actually the dominant path, or whether uniform-state and interpolating diffusion methods scale better once practical sampling tradeoffs are included. The paper stands out for comparing diffusion families at scale, emphasizing FLOPs, likelihood, sampling speed, and downstream quality rather than relying on perplexity alone.

### Core Idea

- Compare masked, uniform-state, and interpolating discrete diffusion language models through scaling-law and speed-quality analyses.
- Show that perplexity can be misleading across diffusion families, since models with worse likelihood scaling may sample faster or perform better in practical settings.
- Scale methods up to 1.7B parameters and report that uniform-state diffusion remains competitive and can outperform AR and masked diffusion baselines on GSM8K after supervised fine-tuning.

### Critical Notes

- Treat the GSM8K result as suggestive rather than a broad reasoning claim until replicated across more tasks and training recipes.
- Check how much the conclusions depend on implementation details, sampler choice, and compute allocation.
- The citation signal is still early because the paper is only from February 2026; the venue/project/code signals matter more right now.

## Scaling Behavior of Discrete Diffusion Language Models

- Link: https://openreview.net/forum?id=GDYaNzxt9T
- Domain: Diffusion language models
- Source: ICLR 2026 poster / OpenReview
- Recommended by: Reasoning & Diffusion LM Paper Scout
- Priority: high
- Topic tags: diffusion-language-models, discrete-diffusion, uniform-diffusion, scaling-laws, compute-efficiency, data-efficiency
- Broader trend: diffusion LM research is shifting from proving that masked denoising can work toward asking which corruption processes and compute allocations actually scale.

### Why This Paper

This is the most useful diffusion LM read today because it studies the scaling behavior of discrete diffusion language models directly rather than only proposing another sampler or benchmark win. Its main value is a controlled comparison of masked and uniform diffusion under scaling-law methodology, with attention to batch size, learning rate, data-versus-parameter allocation, and a 10B-parameter uniform diffusion run at 10^22 FLOPs.

### Core Idea

- Interpolate between masked and uniform discrete diffusion to isolate how the noise process changes scaling behavior.
- Show that uniform diffusion tends to prefer more parameters and less data than masked diffusion under compute-efficient training.
- Recast the discrete diffusion ELBO through signal-to-noise ratio, making the theory and implementation closer to continuous diffusion practice.

### Critical Notes

- The strongest claims are about pretraining loss and scaling behavior, so downstream generation quality and instruction-following transfer still need careful follow-up.
- The result does not imply that uniform diffusion is universally better; it suggests a different compute/data optimum that may matter most in data-constrained regimes.
- Open-source code and model release are listed as acceptance-linked, so reproducibility should be checked against the actual released artifacts.
