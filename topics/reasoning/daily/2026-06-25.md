## V1: Unifying Generation and Self-Verification for Parallel Reasoners

### Recommendation Summary
V1 is worth reading today because it attacks the fragile selection step in parallel test-time scaling: sampling many reasoning traces only helps if the system can reliably identify the best one. The paper's key move is to replace poorly calibrated pointwise self-scoring with pairwise comparison, then train generation and verification together so the verifier tracks the model's evolving output distribution. The result is a practical and well-motivated direction for making inference-time scaling less dependent on majority vote, external judges, or brittle scalar reward calibration.

### Why It Matters Now
Recent reasoning work has moved from "make the model think longer" toward "allocate and verify inference compute intelligently." V1 sits right in that shift: it treats verification not as an afterthought but as a first-class component of the scaling stack, with evaluations across code generation and math reasoning plus public code. It is especially relevant if you care about whether test-time compute gains come from real search/selection improvements rather than just longer traces or prompt-level budget hints.

### Core Idea
- Generate multiple candidate solutions in parallel, then rank them through pairwise self-verification rather than independent scalar scoring.
- Use an uncertainty-guided tournament-style procedure to spend verification calls on candidate pairs whose relative quality is ambiguous.
- Train a single model with V1-PairRL so generation and pairwise verification co-evolve on in-distribution rollouts.

### What To Be Skeptical About
- Pairwise judging may still inherit position bias, shared-model blind spots, or correlated failure modes when all candidates are wrong.
- The strongest gains may depend on candidate diversity; if sampling collapses to similar traces, better ranking has less to exploit.
- The method adds verification calls, so the real value depends on latency/cost curves under production-style batching and model-serving constraints.
