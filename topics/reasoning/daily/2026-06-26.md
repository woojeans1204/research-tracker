## Beyond Magnitude: Leveraging Direction of RLVR Updates for LLM Reasoning

- Link: https://openreview.net/forum?id=r6Pw3RiMYL
- arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.22117
- Domain: LLM reasoning
- Source: scheduled daily recommendation
- Recommended by: Reasoning & Diffusion LM Paper Scout
- Priority: high
- Topic tags: reasoning, RLVR, token-level-analysis, test-time-extrapolation, training-dynamics
- Broader trend: mechanistic and optimization-level understanding of RLVR for reasoning models

### Recommendation Summary
This is today's strongest reasoning read because it moves beyond asking whether RLVR improves benchmark scores and asks what kind of token-level change actually carries the reasoning improvement. The paper argues that the signed direction of the log-probability shift between a base model and an RLVR-trained model is more informative than magnitude-only signals such as entropy or divergence. That makes it useful both as a diagnostic paper and as a bridge from post-hoc RLVR analysis to practical interventions.

### Why It Matters Now
Recent reasoning work is crowded with RLVR recipes, test-time scaling variants, and claims about sparse updates. This paper is worth reading now because it gives a sharper lens for that discussion: if reasoning gains are concentrated in small but directionally meaningful token changes, then better training and inference methods should target those changes rather than merely increasing rollout count or optimizing aggregate rewards. The ICLR 2026 poster signal is strong enough to treat it as more than a transient arXiv curiosity, while citation and community signals are still early.

### Core Idea
- Compare base and RLVR-trained models using signed token-level Delta log p, not just update magnitude.
- Validate that Delta log p identifies reasoning-critical tokens through selective token-replacement interventions.
- Use the same signal for two interventions: test-time extrapolation along the learned direction and training-time reweighting of low-probability, high-impact tokens.

### Evaluation Notes
- Strengths: Central to RLVR reasoning; studies token-level causal-ish interventions rather than only final benchmark deltas; connects analysis to both inference-time and training-time methods; reports improvements across models and benchmarks.
- External validation: OpenReview lists it as an ICLR 2026 Poster, published 2026-01-26 and last modified 2026-04-11. The arXiv version was submitted 2026-03-23.
- Community signal: Hugging Face Daily Papers shows modest early attention, including 29 upvotes and several collection saves, but this is not yet a mature citation signal.

### What To Be Skeptical About
- The token-replacement experiments are informative, but they are still controlled interventions, not proof that the same signal will transfer cleanly to every RLVR setup or domain.
- Delta log p depends on comparing a base and trained policy, so the method is less directly useful when only a deployed black-box reasoning model is available.
- Reported gains should be read against compute, rollout, and benchmark diversity; the most important question is whether the diagnostic remains predictive outside math-heavy, verifiable-reward regimes.
