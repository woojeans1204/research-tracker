## Recommendation Summary

This is worth reading today because it sharpens the test-time scaling question from "can a model think longer?" to "has it learned how to use extra compute for real exploration?" The paper argues that many reasoning models saturate when extrapolated beyond their training token budget, then proposes an RL recipe that encourages in-context exploration by chaining asymmetric skills such as verification, generation, and refinement. Its validation signal is strong for a recent paper: ICLR 2026 Poster status, a focused extrapolation problem, ablations around the training recipe, and high small-model math results on AIME'25 and HMMT'25.

## Why This Paper Is Worth Reading Now

Reasoning research is converging on inference-time compute as the scarce resource to allocate, but a lot of work still equates scaling with longer traces or more samples. e3 is important because it treats exploration behavior itself as something to train, not just prompt or budget-force at inference time. That makes it a useful reference point for judging whether new reasoning methods actually improve search behavior or merely produce longer-looking deliberation.

## Core Idea

- Train models to spend extra test-time tokens on in-context exploration: trying hypotheses, checking them, and refining before committing.
- Exploit asymmetries where verification or critique is easier than generating a correct solution from scratch.
- Use negative gradients from incorrect traces plus a difficulty-token-budget curriculum to make longer reasoning budgets continue to help.

## Broader Trend

Trainable test-time scaling for reasoning: moving from fixed chain-of-thought length, Best-of-N, and prompt-level budget forcing toward models that internalize search, verification, and exploration policies during post-training.

## What To Be Skeptical About

- The headline evidence is strongest in math-reasoning settings; transfer to open-ended planning, tool use, and messy real-world agent tasks remains uncertain.
- The method depends on reliable self-verification asymmetries, which may weaken when the model's verifier-like behavior is poorly calibrated or the task has ambiguous success criteria.
- It improves extrapolation to roughly 2x the training budget in the reported setup; that is meaningful, but not proof of indefinite inference-time scaling.
- OpenReview confirms ICLR 2026 Poster status. Public review text was not visible in the retrieved page, so the review-discussion signal is positive but limited beyond the acceptance signal.
